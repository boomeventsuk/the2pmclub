10 Reasons Your Hen Do Should Start at 2PM

The smartest decision the maid of honour ever made.

Let's be honest about hen dos. The traditional version goes like this: everyone meets at 7pm, someone's already had too much prosecco by 8, the club is terrible, someone loses a shoe, and the bride spends Sunday morning questioning her life choices.

There's a better way. And it starts at 2pm.

1. Everyone Can Actually Make It

No babysitter drama. No 'I can't do late nights anymore.' 2pm on a Saturday is the one time slot the entire group chat can agree on. The plan that actually happens.

2. You Get the Full Night Out Experience

Club production, massive sound system, confetti cannons, four hours of 80s, 90s and 00s anthems. This isn't cocktails in a restaurant. This is a proper event.

3. The Bride Remembers It

Home by 7, still buzzing, actually remembering every moment. Compare that to piecing together the night from blurry photos and a sense of dread.

4. It's Genuinely Affordable

Group tickets from around £10 per person. For 10 people that's £100. Try booking a private booth at a nightclub for that.

5. No Fancy Dress Required

Wear whatever makes you feel brilliant. Sashes optional. L-plates absolutely not required.

6. You Still Get Your Evening

Done by 6pm. Pub garden for a debrief? Dinner? Home for a film? The hen do doesn't have to be the only thing you do that day.

7. The Photos Are Actually Good

Daylight plus professional event lighting means photos you'll actually want to post. Not the 2am flash-face nightclub shots nobody ever shows their mum.

8. The Music is Guaranteed

Every song is one the whole room knows. Mr Brightside, Wannabe, Dancing Queen, Livin' On A Prayer. No DJ playing songs nobody asked for.

9. No Dodgy Venues

THE 2PM CLUB runs in proper venues across the Midlands: The Picturedrome (Northampton), Bedford Esquires, MK11, hmv Empire (Coventry), Hat Factory (Luton), Welford Road Stadium (Leicester). Real venues with real bars.

10. Sunday Belongs to You

The ultimate hen do flex: she had the best afternoon of her life AND woke up feeling amazing. That's a win the whole WhatsApp group will be talking about.
